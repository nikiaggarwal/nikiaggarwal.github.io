FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Everything you need to know about The Mamdani Meter and how we track promises.

Who created this project?

A Brooklynite who voted for Mamdani in the primary and general.

Is this affiliated with City Hall?

No. The Mamdani Meter is fully independent and not connected to the Mayor's Office, City Hall, or any political organization.

Are you rooting for success or failure?

The project is rooting for tangible results and true accountability. Its purpose is to help New Yorkers understand what was promised, what has happened, and what remains unfinished.

What promises are included?

The project tracks commitments that were specific and voiced on Mamdani's campaign platform. Broad aspirational statements without measurable targets are generally not included unless they were tied to specific numerical goals, legislation, or timelines in campaign materials.

What promises are you not tracking?

To make this site useful and easy for readers to process, I did not include tactical or process-oriented promises that were primarily steps toward a larger policy goal. For example, Mamdani pledged to re-open the Mayor's Office to Protect Tenants on day 1 (which he did end up doing), but that action served his broader commitment to ban landlords with repeat violations. Thus, the action of reopening the office is considered a progress step towards banning landlords with repeat violations.

What about new priorities that weren't in the campaign platform?

Those are generally outside the scope of this project. The Mamdani Meter centers the commitments presented to voters during the campaign, allowing for a clearer assessment of follow-through over time.

What counts as "progress"?

Progress is recorded only when a formal government action occurs that materially advances a promise, such as funding being allocated, legislation moving forward, rules being implemented, or programs launching. Public engagement, speeches, or statements of intent do not count without formal action.

What do the status categories mean?

Each promise is assigned one of five status levels:

  • Delivered — The promise has been fully fulfilled.
  • Making Progress — Meaningful steps have been taken toward fulfillment.
  • Early Days — The promise is acknowledged but action is still in early stages.
  • Headwinds — Progress has stalled or faces significant obstacles.
  • Nope — The promise has been broken or abandoned.

Status labels are of course a simplification. Policy progress is not always linear and it can stall for all kinds of reasons. Status labels are an easy shorthand and you're encouraged to read the full context and updates on each promise page for a complete picture.

Can I make a suggestion?

Absolutely! I welcome input and assistance from the community. Please reach out to us at hello@themamdanimeter.com with your feedback.

Can you personally tell me when there are progress updates, so I don't have to keep checking the site?

Sure thing! Sign up for this free newsletter, and I will email you once a week with an update.
