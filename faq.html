FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

The stuff you should know before trusting a random website to tell you how your mayor is doing.

Who created this project?

A Brooklynite who voted for Mamdani in the primary and general, and then couldn't stop thinking about whether and how any of it would actually happen. This site is the result of that particular affliction.

Is this affiliated with City Hall?

Nope. The Mamdani Meter is fully independent. No ties to the Mayor's Office, City Hall, or any political organization. Just a civilian with a spreadsheet and strong opinions.

What promises are included?

The project tracks commitments that were specific and voiced on Mamdani's campaign platform. Broad aspirational statements without measurable targets are generally not included unless they were tied to specific numerical goals, legislation, or timelines in campaign materials.

What promises are you not tracking?

Tactical steps that exist mainly to serve a bigger goal. For example: Mamdani pledged to reopen the Mayor's Office to Protect Tenants on day one (which he did), but that action is really just scaffolding for his broader promise to ban landlords with repeat violations. So it lives as a progress update on that promise, not its own line item. There are limitations to this approach, but it keeps things from getting unwieldy.

What about new priorities that weren't in the campaign platform?

Out of scope. The purpose of this project is to measure follow-through on the specific policies that New Yorkers voted for. New priorities are definitely worth tracking (just not here).

What counts as "progress"?

Formal government action that materially moves a promise forward: funding allocated, legislation advancing, rules implemented, programs launching, etc. Speeches, press releases, and stated intentions don't count until something actually happens.

What do the status categories mean?
  • Delivered — Done. Uncork the champagne.
  • Making Progress — Meaningful steps taken. Things are moving.
  • Early Days — Action is still getting off the ground. The clock is running.
  • Headwinds — Stalled or facing real obstacles. Could be Albany. Could be the budget. Could be both.
  • Nope — Broken or abandoned. Oof.

A quick note: these labels are a shorthand, not a verdict. Policy progress is messy and nonlinear, and a "Headwinds" today can become "Making Progress" next month. Always worth reading the full context on each promise page before drawing conclusions.

Can I make a suggestion?

Please do! If I'm missing something, got something wrong, or you have context I don't, reach out: hello@themamdanimeter.com

Can you personally tell me when there are progress updates, so I don't have to keep checking the site?

Sure thing! Sign up for this free newsletter, and I will email you once a week with an update.
